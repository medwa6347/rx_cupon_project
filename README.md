This project assessed how benefit-plan changes tied to out-of-pocket accumulator programs affect patients using manufacturer copay assistance, with a focus on understanding the size of the impacted population and the downstream effects on deductible fulfillment, medication adherence/persistence, utilization, and coupon spend. The study identified eligible patients with products of interest, linked them to family members and benefit design, pulled medical and pharmacy claims, and compared outcomes across deductible and non-deductible plans with and without OOP accumulator programs. It also included counterfactual analysis to estimate how much additional manufacturer coupon spending was attributable to these newer benefit designs
